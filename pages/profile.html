Background

Growing up in a quiet town in Kogi State, my relationship with technology began in a very personal and creative way. We had a computer at home, not as a tool for technical exploration, but as a canvas for my imagination. My early years were spent typing out the stories I wrote, sketching houses and other things with digital paintbrushes and getting lost in the simple joy of games. The internet and the mechanics of websites were distant concepts; the computer was simply a portal for my creativity and a source of entertainment.
As I got older, I became more curious. I started to wonder how I could share the stories and drawings I made with other people. This made me look at the internet in a new way. My early lack of interest changed into a strong curiosity. I began to learn how websites are made, how information travels online, and how everything works together. This is how I went from just using a computer, to being passionate about building things in the digital world.

Education

I hold a degree in Human Anatomy, which at first seemed to have nothing to do with web development. Because of this, I was very afraid that I couldn't follow my interest in technology. I worried that without a strong tech background, web development would be too difficult and completely different from the human body I had studied for years.
However, everything changed on the very first day of my web development class. The tutor began by introducing the three core components of a website: HTML, which provides the basic structure; CSS, which handles the styling; and JavaScript, which controls the behavior. To my surprise, we used an analogy that immediately connected with me: the human body. The tutor explained that HTML is like the human skeleton, providing the fundamental structure or framework. CSS is like the external features of the body which includes; the skin, hair, and clothing, giving it its appearance. Finally, JavaScript is like the nervous system and brain, controlling the body's movements and interactions.
In that moment, web development made more sense for me. I realized that my background in anatomy had actually given me a unique way to understand this new field. My fear disappeared and I saw that my past experience wasn't a weakness at all, but an unexpected strength.
